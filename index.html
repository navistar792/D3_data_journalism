Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Obesity

In general, the plot shows a correlation between poverty rate and obesity; states with higher poverty levels show higher rates of obesity.

Limiting our analysis to high poverty rates (above 18), many states from the Southeast region appear. States like Mississipi, Louisiana, Arkansas, Alabama, and West virginia have the highest rates of obesity and poverty. A notable exception includes California, which shows one of the lowest rates of obesity, but a moderately high rate of poverty.
